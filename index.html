Why I built this

My grandmother fell. Nobody knew for hours.

She lived alone. She was independent, sharp, proud. One morning she slipped in the kitchen and couldn't get up. By the time a neighbor noticed, hours had passed.

My parents still live together, but my father often goes for long walks in the forest or hunting — leaving my mother home alone for hours. And when he's out there, he's on his own too. I know them — neither would wear a medical alert pendant, they hate check-in calls that make them feel like a burden, and cameras would strip their dignity.

Elderly people don't want to feel watched. They don't want to press buttons. They just want to live their lives.

So I built something different. An app that sits quietly on their phone, learns what a normal day looks like, and emails you only when something seems wrong. No hardware. No interaction. They don't even know it's there.

"I don't do it for the money. I just want to help people like my parents — and yours."

Common questions

Frequently Asked Questions

What emergencies can it detect?

Unusual stillness (potential falls or medical emergencies), abnormally long sleep, and location deviations from established safe zones. The AI learns what's normal for YOUR parent specifically.

How does the 7-day learning phase work?

The AI observes daily patterns — when they wake up, activity levels, regular locations. Basic monitoring is active from Day 1, but personalized thresholds are refined by Day 7. Weekly recalibration continues to adapt as their routine changes.

Is my parent's data safe?

All detailed data stays on their phone. For safety analysis, anonymous behavioral summaries are sent securely to Google's AI — no names or personal identifiers, ever. Emergency alerts go via encrypted email to family members.

What if the app sends false alerts?

The AI continuously learns and adapts. False alerts decrease over time. The app also adapts to travel — instead of 7 days of false alarms during a vacation, you get one "they're traveling" notification.

How much does it cost?

Free to download with a 21-day trial. Then $49/year for the first year, just $5/year after that. No hardware, no call center — just intelligent software on the phone they already carry. Cancel anytime.

What if the phone manufacturer kills the app?

The app has built-in self-healing — multiple recovery layers automatically restart monitoring. If it can't recover, it emails your family with a simple fix. Silent push notifications every 6 hours help keep monitoring alive even on aggressive manufacturers like Samsung, Xiaomi, and Honor.
